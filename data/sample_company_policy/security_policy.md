# Information Security Policy

## Account Security

Employees must use a unique password for every company account. Passwords must
contain at least 14 characters, and company passwords must not be reused for
personal services.

Multi-factor authentication is required wherever the company provides it.
Employees must never approve an authentication request they did not initiate.

## Data Handling

Confidential information may be stored only in company-approved systems.
Employees must not send confidential files to personal email accounts or copy
them to personal cloud storage.

Sensitive documents must be shared only with people who need access for their
work. Printed confidential material must be collected promptly and disposed of
in secure recycling bins.

## Networks and Devices

Employees must use the company VPN when accessing company systems from public
or shared Wi-Fi. Only company-managed devices may be used to access
confidential systems.

Employees must lock their screens whenever they leave a device unattended,
even for a short period.

## Reporting Security Incidents

Suspected phishing, accidental data disclosure, lost devices, stolen devices,
and unusual account activity must be reported to the Security team immediately
through the security incident form or the emergency security phone number.

Employees should not delay reporting while attempting to investigate the issue
themselves. The Security team will coordinate containment and next steps.

## Phishing Messages

Employees who receive a suspicious message should not click links, open
attachments, reply, or forward it to colleagues. They should use the email
client's **Report Phishing** button and then delete the message when instructed
by the Security team.
