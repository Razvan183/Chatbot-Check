# Employee Onboarding Guide

## Before the Start Date

HR sends new employees their employment paperwork and first-day schedule by
email. New employees should complete required forms at least 2 business days
before their start date.

The hiring manager requests the employee's system access and equipment. IT
prepares standard equipment for collection or delivery.

## First Day

New employees should arrive at 09:00 unless HR provides a different time.
The first day includes identity verification, an office orientation, equipment
setup, and a meeting with the direct manager.

Remote employees join the orientation by video and should confirm delivery of
their equipment with IT.

## Required Training

Security awareness and data-protection training must be completed within the
first 5 working days. Code-of-conduct training must be completed within the
first 10 working days.

Managers are responsible for assigning role-specific training and introducing
the new employee to their team.

## Introductory Period

New employees have a 90-day introductory period. The manager should hold
check-in meetings near the end of the employee's first 30, 60, and 90 days.

The introductory period supports feedback and goal setting. It does not change
the employee's obligation to follow company policies from the first day.

## Getting Help

Questions about employment records or leave should be sent to HR. Technical
issues and equipment requests should be sent to the IT Help Desk. Suspected
security incidents must be reported directly to the Security team.
