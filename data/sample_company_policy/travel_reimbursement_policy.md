# Business Travel and Reimbursement Policy

## Approval and Booking

Business travel must be approved by the employee's manager before booking.
Employees should use the company's designated booking provider whenever it
offers a reasonable route.

Economy class is required for flights shorter than 6 hours. Premium economy may
be approved for flights lasting 6 hours or longer. Business-class travel
requires written approval from the Chief Operating Officer.

## Accommodation

Hotel costs are reimbursable up to €150 per night, including taxes. A manager
may approve a higher amount before booking when no safe, reasonable option is
available within the limit.

Employees must submit an itemized hotel receipt.

## Meals and Local Transport

Meals are reimbursable up to a total of €35 per day during approved business
travel. Alcohol is not reimbursable.

Reasonable public transport, taxi, or ride-hailing costs between the airport,
hotel, office, and customer location are reimbursable. A receipt is required
for any individual transport expense above €20.

## Expense Submission

Expense claims must be submitted through the expense system within 15 calendar
days after the trip ends. Receipts are required for individual expenses above
€10 unless this policy states a stricter requirement.

Expenses submitted after 15 days require a written explanation and may be
rejected by Finance.

## Travel Incidents

If company equipment is lost or stolen during travel, the employee must follow
the reporting process in the Equipment Policy and the Security Policy. Any
suspected theft should also be reported to local authorities when it is safe to
do so.
